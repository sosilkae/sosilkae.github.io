International Women's Day

Young Women in Business SFU

Event and program design for YWiB’s 6th Annual International Women’s Day Conference.

Service: Print, Graphic Design

Roles: Art Direction, Ideation, Branding, Visual Design

Programs used: Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign

As YWiB’s Media Design Coordinator (2015/2016), my role was to lead, design, and execute the visual brand identity for this milestone event. A multi-page brochure was developed as the event’s program guide for attendees. I also produced a range of digital and print promotional media leading up to the conference including digital ads, posters, and flyers.

Responsibilities

This role included working closely with the Event Project Manager to ensure the program package included everything as needed. In order to get a better sense of the event, we discussed the tone of the event, and what the differences were from the previous year. Taking this feedback, I created a moodboard to express these intentions into a visual form, and presented it back to the coordinators. In comparison to the program guide I produced in the previous year, I challenged myself to use less white space and create a more vivid voice by contrasting colours within the brand palette.

Challenges

A challenge I faced was aligning the existing brand colours to the new visual propositions for the event. There was a need for a lighter atmosphere, however YWiB’s brand palettes featured hard and vibrant pinks, purples and white. To solve this, I drafted iterations of colour stories with different levels of pinks and purples to see how I could create the same contrasting effects as single vibrant colours. As a result, I was able to combine pastel pink, dusty pink, and a purple periwinkle together while using the same brand colour tones.
