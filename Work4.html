Western Union Messenger

UX / UI Project

Reducing anxiety around the money transferring experience.

Roles: User Experience, Visual Design, Prototyping

Programs used: Illustrator, Sketch, Principle

Background

Western Union's money transfer infrastructure is a complicated, outdated system that competes against both established transfer apps and other new faces in the payment sector. Their process is cluttered and can be emotionally taxing for its users.

Insight

Talking About Money Begins with a Conversation

What if we can alleviate the stress and anxiety of money transfer from the start? If communication and transfer processes can seamlessly work together in one channel, we can reduce anxieties around the subject and create a natural atmosphere around finances.

Proposal

Sending Money is as Easy as Connecting

Western Union Messenger--a messaging app connecting families separated by great distances together, with money transfer seamlessly integrated into the background.

Target Audience

Western Union Messenger is for the migrant workers in rapidly growing areas, like Dubai, who are sending money to their family back home. In 2012, the migrant worker sent home total of $526 billion in remittance, and this number is set to cross the $700 billion mark in 2016.

Western Union Messenger gives us the perfect opportunity to solve a larger issue regarding emotional welfare and communication in the lives of migrant workers. With constant stress and responsibility to take care of the family, the addition of handling money transfers becomes a process full of unnecessary anxiety, miscommunication, and general confusion.

Let's Talk Money

Money is Integrated into the Conversation

Western Union’s current process only focuses on the money sending aspect. However there are gaps in between where the user needs to seek other means of communication to contact the person they are sending money to. We saw this as an opportunity to fill in the touch-points in between the process to ease anxiety of the user and create a seamless experience.

Initiate Transfers in 2 Ways

1 -- The Money Button

It’s part of the language. Use the money button on the keyboard to send or request a transfer without having to leave the conversation.

2 –- Embedded Quick Links

Typing in a number with a currency symbol creates these convenient moments in chat to commence a transfer.

Making the Money Transferring Process Feel Less like a Transaction

Instead of punching in their pin like at an ATM, using a swipe code protector is still a familiar way to feel secure when going through a process. It helps the recipient feel more immersed in the experience.

Reducing the Friction in the Pick-Up Process

1 -- Utilize Existing Stores

Western Union differentiates itself from competitors with its global reach of physical store locations in more than 200 countries. Recipients are able to receive transfers in cash if they don’t have a bank account--however, customers may face unexpected additional processes upon visiting a location. We wanted to tackle this friction point so we created a scannable, password-protected page in which customers can use as verification when they pick up a money transfer--removing frustrations around the hassle, stress, and time spent in-store.

2 –- Transaction History Filter

Easily filter through transactions or archived messages to find what you need. Using a filter as a transaction history log helps provide a way to make sure all your money-related information doesn’t get lost in the conversation.
