Melancholy Pin

Personal Project

Designed hard enamel pins and card illustrations, with 10% of proceeds going to the Canadian Mental Health Association.

Role: Illustration, Graphic Design, Print, Production

Programs used: Illustrator

Purpose

After the occasional monthly burst of wanting-to-make-a-difference-in-the-world, I decided to finally act on that energy. I wanted to give to a cause I resonated with, and in some way share that with people I knew. My goal was to produce something of my own from start to finish, and share the profit with an organisation of my choice, the Canadian Mental Health Association.

This pin is an illustration of a girl blanketed by her own hair. She seems contemplative, pensive, or perhaps even anxious. The idea I wanted to express was a figure submerged in their own self--a way for them to take time to reflect and reevaluate. I also designed cards with linescape visuals to complement the submerged-like illustration

Learning Something New

Sourcing manufacturers was a unique experience, and something I had never done before. I wanted to challenge myself and try to see through an entire a low-scale production project. This project included researching the product needs, reaching out to different manufacturers, and learning the basics of setting up a small e-commerce website.

Casual Marketing

I used Instagram as my main source of advertising. I started with sneak peek Instagram Stories to see who was interested, and got a lot of feedback! Though my goal was to sell them casually to friends and family, I later decided to set up an e-commerce website to cater to the interest in my social network.

Surprises

I originally ordered 50 pieces, but after a couple sneakpeek posts I received a lot of interest, and sold out of pins before I could put them on my e-commerce website. I currently am in the process and running a re-order, and will be conducting pre-sale orders.

Takeaway

As one of my first personal projects, I am very touched to see so many of my friends and followers express their interest in the pin and the cause. Although this is a very small step to making a contribution to the world, it was something I had never done and shared with people before. I am proud of being able to share my work with others, and how this aligns with how I want to continue to do work with a purpose bigger than myself.

If you’re interested in collaborating, have a new project in mind, or want to chat, get in touch with me! I’d love to hear from you.
